About Ethereum

Ethereum is a blockchain-based software platform that can be used for sending and receiving value globally with its native cryptocurrency, ether, without any third-party interference.

First proposed in 2013 by Russian-Canadian computer programmer Vitalik Buterin, Ethereum was designed to expand the utility of cryptocurrencies by allowing developers to create their own special applications. Unlike traditional apps, these Ethereum-based applications, called “decentralized applications,” or dapps, are self-executing thanks to the use of smart contracts.

Daily Price Prediction Model

The Daily Price Prediction Model is an LSTM Recurrent Neural Network model that predicts the next day ETH closing price. It takes the last 60 days of closing prices, feeds that into the LSTM model as a time-series input, and then predicts the next day's closing price. The above plot shows the true ETH closing price (blue) compared to the model predictions (red)


Daily Price Acceleration Prediction Model


The Daily Price Acceleration Prediction Model is an LSTM Recurrent Neural Network model that predicts the next day ETH price acceleration. The price acceleration is defined as the second derivative of closing price with respect to time (first derivative of price velocity). The last 30 days of closing prices, 20-day moving averages, 50-day moving averages, closing price velocities, and closing price accelerations are fed into the LSTM model as a time-series input, and then predicts the next day's closing price acceleration. The above plot shows the true price acceleration (red) compared to the model predictions (blue).
